Lasagna-Stuffed Shells

The Perfect Family Dish in the Perfect Portions

A perfect blend of ricotta, mozzarella, spinach, and ground beef round out this family friendly recipe. Most lasagnas are not family friendly as they have been known to pull out a switchblade on your children and as everyone is running around, screaming "I didn't know Italian food was sentient!" and "Where did it get a switchblade?!" you'll be glad you made your lasagna with shells. Which are after all a defensive item. No knife needed, not even to portion out an individual serving. Lasagna-Stuffed Shells for the win!

Ingredients

  • Ground Beef: 1 & 1/2 lbs
  • Salt & Ground Black Pepper (to taste)
  • Olive Oil: 2 tbsp, divided
  • Chopped Onion: 2 tbsp
  • Tomato Paste: 2 tbsp
  • Oregano: 1/2 tsp, dried
  • Tomato Sauce: 2 - 14 oz cans, divided
  • Jumbo Pasta Shells: 18 pcs
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, thinly sliced
  • Ricotta Cheese, Whole-Milk: 16 oz
  • Mozzarella Cheese: 16 oz, shredded
  • Spinach, Frozen: 8 oz, chopped/thawed/drained
  • Basil: 1/4 cup, fresh
  • Lemon, Medium: 1, zested/juiced
  • Parmesan Cheese: 1/2 cup, grated

Directions

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add 1/2 the Ground Beef, season with Pepper and a liberal pinch of Salt. Cook for approx. 5 minutes until beef is seared and crumbly. Remove beef, repeat for remainder, then set all meat aside.
  2. Lower heat to medium-low. Add 1 tbsp of Olive Oil and Chopped Onion. Saute approx. 2 minutes or until aromatic. Add Tomato Paste and Oregano, then add Ground Beef and Tomato Sauce: 1 can. Stir to combine and reduce to a simmer. Add Salt and Pepper to taste.
  3. Preheat oven to 375℉/190℃.
  4. Bring large pot of salted water to boil. Add Shells and cook for approx. 9 minutes. Shells should be tender yet firm to the bite. Strain. *note: pasta water can be used later to add to the sauce.
  5. Heat remaining tbsp of Olive Oil in another pan over medium heat, add Garlic. Saute until lightly golden. Transfer garlic to mixing bowl with Ricotta, Mozzarella, Spinach, Basil, and Lemon Zest & Juice. Add Salt to taste.
  6. Pour remaining Tomato Sauce in a 9x13-inch baking dish. Spoon cheese mixture into shells, followed by meat. Place shell into sauce when stuffed, top with Parmesan Cheese.
  7. Bake in preheated oven approx. 25 - 30 minutes or until shells are hot in the center and the mozzarella cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Remove dish from oven and let cool until ready to serve. Serve and enjoy knowing you've escaped the tyranny of non-friendly lasagna and the horrors they would inflict upon your loved ones in their zealous quest for ever-growing control over the minds of the people.
